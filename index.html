The answer in 30 seconds

Attractive market. Unforgiving acquisition math.

The opportunity is real, but the market size is not the decision. The decision is whether Core sells repeatably and paid media expands revenue without destroying trust or margin.

The balanced case

Why it is worth testing. Why it can still fail.

The research supports a pilot, not conviction theater. Both sides of the case are strong enough to deserve operating gates.

What makes HVAC compelling

Structural fit between the market and the founders' capability set.

  • Large, fragmented market with a clear 21-24K in-band employer target.
  • Google-mediated demand makes spend waste and missed calls visible in dollars.
  • Google Search and LSA create a natural $499-$699 expansion path after Core earns trust.
  • A real pricing gap exists between $249 template mills and $2,800+ agencies.
  • AI fulfillment can bundle more value at a one-product price.
  • Buyer pain is concrete: hostage assets, lock-ins, bad leads, and unanswered phones.
  • The website rebuild can show proof before trust exists.

What can kill the thesis

The failure modes attack sales, churn, compliance, and founder focus.

  • Owners already pattern-match "we reviewed your website" as spam.
  • The sourced funnel supports 4-5 closes per rep; that is viable at $2K salary, but client fit and retention are still unproven.
  • 2026 demand is correcting while 53% prioritize current customers over acquisition.
  • ServiceTitan and Avoca compress the high-spend tier.
  • Paid media raises the burden from shipping assets to proving qualified calls and booked jobs.
  • TCPA discipline can throttle the dial volume the model depends on.
  • Two founders running two verticals can produce an ambiguous, under-resourced test.

Market and buyer

Qualification beats persuasion.

The 120K-business headline is not the sales list. The target is a visible-spend minority already paying for Google, Angi, or an agency and still leaking value.

Product architecture

The website opens the door. Operations justify the subscription.

Do not sell a website retainer. Land with owned infrastructure, then expand into Google Search, LSA, and Meta only when tracking can tie spend to qualified calls and booked jobs.

The website is the wedge - not the product, not irrelevant.

A personalized rebuild attacks the first 30 seconds of a cold sale. It becomes credible only when it names the prospect's reviews, photos, license, market, and ownership terms in a human-voiced walkthrough.

Sequencing and goals

Now is preparation season. September is decision season.

The clean sequence protects the smoke-shop warm-book advantage while creating a valid HVAC test with dedicated people, capacity, compliance, and instrumentation.

  • Measure artifact throughput and live-call generation time.
  • Build Google Ads, LSA, Meta, call-tracking, and offline-conversion SOPs.

SEP 01, 2026

Open with two net-new pilot reps.

Separate brand, one internal platform, TSI-orphan and agency-switcher lists first.

  • Core bundle at $349-$449 plus $399-$500 setup.
  • Offer Google Growth only where ad spend, tracking, and intake capacity are credible.
  • Artifact engagement instrumented from day one.
  • 7:30-8:00 AM local calling window and multi-touch cadence.
DEC 01, 2026

Read G1 and G2 without excuses.

At roughly $7K-$11K modeled exposure, decide whether the artifact and close motion are working.

  • If engagement fails, the artifact/list/message thesis failed.
  • If engagement passes but closes fail, price, terms, or rep execution failed.
  • Measure Google Growth attach separately; do not rescue weak Core sales with assumed upsell.
MAR 01, 2027

Read retention and attach.

Determine whether onboarding, booked-job reporting, and paid-media attach create a durable account without widening the Core-only loss envelope.

  • Churn and Google Growth attach arms of G3 are binding.
  • Two gate failures trigger exit or B2B2B pivot.

Research integrity

The audit made the recommendation harder - and more useful.

The briefing keeps the paper's retractions, derived estimates, and unknowns visible. This is a decision tool, not a sales deck.

Material corrections
  • LSA dispute recovery was withdrawn because Google removed manual disputes in July 2024.
  • The old conservative cash-positive claim was arithmetically impossible and withdrawn.
  • The target-band count was reduced to 21-24K and labeled as derived.
  • The founder-provided rep assumption changes from the paper's $8K loaded estimate to a $2K monthly salary.
  • Under that override and the actual $349-$449 Core range, planning reaches cash-positive around months 6-8 and one-call-close around month 4.
What remains unverified
  • Solicitations per owner per week.
  • Published conversion data for full-rebuild-before-contact outreach.
  • HVAC-specific subscription churn.
  • Whether a $500 setup fee depresses phone close rates.
  • Google Growth and Meta attach rates, delivery hours, and gross margin.
  • Minimum client ad spend required to produce a readable campaign signal.
  • Exact HVAC-only employer counts within the Census band.
Falsification triggers
  • A competitor running personalized video rebuilds at more than 1,000 per month kills the white-space premise.
  • Artifact conversation rate below 3% kills the artifact thesis.
  • Two failed gates trigger exit or channel pivot.
  • Smoke warm-book performance missing by more than 30% delays HVAC to the February-April 2027 window.
